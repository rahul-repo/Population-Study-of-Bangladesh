\newline The animation of the population pyramid across years reveals how the demographic structure of a country evolves over time.  

\subsection*{Early Years (Expansive Pyramid)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item The base of the pyramid is wide, indicating \textbf{high birth rates}.  
    \item A large proportion of the population is concentrated in the younger age groups (0--14 years).  
    \item This suggests limited healthcare, high fertility, and possibly higher infant mortality.  
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Middle Years (Transitional Pyramid)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item The base begins to \textbf{narrow}, showing that birth rates are declining.  
    \item The middle of the pyramid (working-age population, 15--64 years) \textbf{expands}, reflecting improvements in healthcare, education, and economic opportunities.  
    \item This stage often corresponds to the \textbf{demographic dividend}, when the working-age population outnumbers dependents, boosting economic growth potential.  
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Recent Years (Constrictive Pyramid)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item The top of the pyramid widens, representing \textbf{increasing life expectancy} and a growing elderly population (65+ years).  
    \item The base becomes relatively narrow, showing \textbf{low fertility rates} and slower population growth.  
    \item This signals an \textbf{aging population}, which can create challenges such as increased demand for healthcare, pensions, and social support.  
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Overall Trend}
The transition from a wide-based to a narrow-based pyramid reflects the \textbf{demographic transition model}:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Stage 1--2:} High fertility and mortality.  
    \item \textbf{Stage 3:} Falling fertility, rising life expectancy.  
    \item \textbf{Stage 4--5:} Aging population, low fertility, possible population decline.  
\end{enumerate}
